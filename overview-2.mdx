**Reports** are a core feature on the Playlog platform that provide comprehensive performance tracking for your Out-of-Home (OOH) campaigns. Acting as the central source of truth for media buyers and agencies, this feature translates complex, hyper-local mobility data into clear, actionable business insights.

Whether a campaign is actively running or has already concluded, you can utilize this feature to obtain detailed reports on your actual real-world delivery, ranging from daily impressions and rich audience demographics to verified Proof of Play.

This page will help you understand the various sections and data points available within the Campaign Reports feature.

## Playlog Measurement

The foundation of OOH measurement globally relies on three primary metrics. These metrics interact with each other to determine the total campaign delivery, which is calculated simply as: **Reach x Frequency = Impression**.

![Measurement Metrics 1](/images/measurement-metrics-1.png)

### Reach

The number of unique individuals that will likely see your ads regardless of frequency.

**Example:** Since 100 different people passed through the area, the **Reach = 10,000**.

### Frequency

Is the average number of times the ads are seen by the unique population reached.

**Example:** Because the average audience see the billboard 4 times, the **Frequency = 4**.

### Impression

The total number of exposures to your advertisement.

**Example (Calculation):**

- Impression = Reach x Frequency
- Impression = 100 (people) x 4 (views)
- **Impression = 4,000**

## Campaign Reports Measurement Overview

![Campaign Report Measurement Overview](/images/campaign-report-measurement-overview.png)

Based on the **Campaign Reports Measurement Overview** above, here is the explanation for each section:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Campaign Name and Period">
    Information regarding the name of the campaign and the campaign period (duration).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share Campaign Report Button">
    This button is used to generate a web link that you can then provide to your clients, allowing them to access the campaign report directly via a web browser (see Share Campaign Report).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download Campaign Report Button">
    This button is used to **download** the desired campaign report. The successfully downloaded report will be in **PDF file** format (see Download Campaign Report).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by Date Range">
    The date range that can be used on this page is the range from the campaign schedule you previously applied. By **default**, the selected date range in this filter is **1 month backward** (if the campaign period is longer than 1 month).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by Media Plan">
    The data displayed on this page can be filtered based on the **Media Plan**. If a single campaign contains multiple media plans, you can specifically choose to display the campaign report only for a certain **Media Plan.**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by City & District">
    The data displayed on this page can be filtered based on the **City or District** where the Inventory point is located.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by Inventory">
    The data displayed on this page can be filtered based on the specific **Inventory** item you want.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter Menu">
    Through the filter menu, you can perform filtering or **refining of campaign reporting data** to better suit your needs. The filters available include:

    - **Filter by Date Range**
    - **Filter by Media Plan**
    - **Filter by Inventory**
    - **Filter by Display Type**
    - **Filter by Inventory Size**

    Once you have finished filtering, press the **‘Apply Filter’** button, and the campaign report will display data based on your specific requirements.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filtering Search Button">
    After you have performed the filtering (Points No. 4, 5, 6, and 7), you can press this search button, and the campaign report will display data based on your specific requirements.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Media Plan Information">
    The total number of media plans contained within a campaign.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Media Plan Information](/images/campaign-report-measurement-media-plan-information.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Inventory Information">
    The total number of inventory items contained within a campaign.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Inventory Information](/images/campaign-report-measurement-inventory-information.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Total Campaign Duration Information">
    The total duration of a campaign. The unit used is days.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Total Duration](/images/campaign-report-measurement-total-duration.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Total Remaining Campaign Days Information">
    The total **remaining duration** of a campaign. The unit used is **days**.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Remaining Days](/images/campaign-report-measurement-remaining-days.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Overview Tab">
    The currently active tab presents the main dashboard view with high-level performance charts and metrics.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Overview Tab 1](/images/campaign-report-measurement-overview-tab-1.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Documentations Tab">
    A secondary tab likely used for viewing campaign evidence, such as proof-of-play photos or installation documents (see Upload Proof of Play)

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Documentation Tab](/images/campaign-report-measurement-documentation-tab.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Average Performance / Hour">
    A bar and line chart detailing the average Impression, Reach, and Frequency metrics broken down by the 24 hours of a day, helping identify peak traffic times.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Average Performance Hour](/images/campaign-report-measurement-average-performance-hour.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Average Performance / Day">
    A chart displaying how the performance metrics average out across the different days of the week (Sunday through Saturday).

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Average Performance Day](/images/campaign-report-measurement-average-performance-day.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Show Data By Toggle">
    A control that allows you to adjust the time interval of the daily performance chart, switching the view between Month, Week, or Day.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Filter Data](/images/campaign-report-measurement-filter-data.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Total Impression">
    A key summary box showing the cumulative advertisement exposures generated during the selected period.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Total Impression](/images/campaign-report-measurement-total-impression.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Average Impression">
    Displays the average number of impressions the campaign successfully generates.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Avg Dayy](/images/campaign-report-measurement-avg-dayy.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Highest Impression">
    Highlights the peak performance, showing the maximum number of impressions recorded.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Highest Day](/images/campaign-report-measurement-highest-day.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Lowest Impression">
    Shows the minimum number of impressions recorded.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Lowest Day](/images/campaign-report-measurement-lowest-day.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Daily Performance Chart">
    A comprehensive area chart tracking the continuous trend of Impression, Reach, and Frequency over the specific dates of the campaign.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Daily Performance Chart](/images/campaign-report-measurement-daily-performance-chart.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Inventory Performance Table">
    A detailed data grid listing each individual billboard screen, displaying its physical size, Total Impressions, Average Impressions Per Day, and pagination controls to navigate the list.

    ![Campaign Report Measurement Inventory Performance Table 1](/images/campaign-report-measurement-inventory-performance-table-1.png)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Campaign Reports Demographic Overview

![Campaign Report Demographic Overview](/images/campaign-report-demographic-overview.png)

Based on the **Campaign Reports Demographic Overview** above, here is the explanation for each section:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Age Demographics">
    A bar chart breaking down the audience exposed to your campaign into specific age groups (e.g., 15-19, 20-24, 25-29).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Gender Demographic">
    A pie chart displaying the gender breakdown (such as the percentage of Male and Female viewers) of the audience reached by the campaign.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Home Location">
    A pie chart that identifies the primary residential areas, cities, or home districts of the audiences who have seen your campaign.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Office Location">
    A pie chart illustrating the primary working areas or office districts of your campaign's audience.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Education Demographic">
    A pie chart detailing the highest level of education attained by the audience (e.g., SLTA/High School, S1/Bachelor's Degree).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Occupation Demographic">
    A pie chart showing the primary professions or job categories of the people exposed to the campaign (e.g., Wiraswasta/Entrepreneurs, Pelajar/Students, PNS/Civil Servants).
  </Step>
  <Step title="SES Segment">
    A pie chart providing a breakdown of the audience's Socio-Economic Status or economic class (e.g., Upper A, Middle B), which helps you gauge their overall purchasing power.
  </Step>
</Steps>
