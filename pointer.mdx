title: "Pointer & Direction Facing"
---

When navigating the interactive map within the Explore module, the system uses specific visual indicators to help you understand not just where a billboard is located, but exactly how it will be seen by the audience in the real world.

![Pointer And Facing Overview](/images/pointer-and-facing-overview.png)

Here is a detailed breakdown of the Pointer icons and the Direction Facing visualizer:

## The Pointer (Inventory Pin)

The pointers are the circular icons scattered across the map. Each pointer represents the exact physical geographic coordinates (latitude and longitude) of a billboard structure.

![Pointer](/images/pointer.png)

**Precise Location**

As you zoom in on the map, these pointers allow you to pinpoint the exact placement of the asset down to the street level, showing whether it is on a specific intersection, along a highway, or near a point of interest.

**Asset Identification**

The icons inside the pointers often provide a quick visual cue about the asset itself, such as whether it is a portrait screen, a landscape digital billboard, or a multi-screen structure.

---

## Direction Facing and the Viewing Zone

When you click on a specific inventory pointer on the map, a transparent blue cone or polygon will project outward from the pin. This shape is a highly detailed representation of the billboard's "Direction Facing" and its actual "Viewing Zone."

![Facing](/images/facing.png)

This blue area provides critical insights for media planning:

- **Direction Facing:** The direction the cone points reveals exactly which way the front of the screen is facing. This is vital for determining which flow of traffic will see your ad. For example, a screen located on a North-South highway might be facing South, meaning it targets northbound traffic.
- **Maximum Noting Distance:** The length or furthest edge of the blue cone represents the maximum distance from which an average person can clearly read and comprehend the advertisement. It visualizes the screen's real-world legibility range.
- **Angle to the Road:** The shape of the cone shows how the physical screen is angled relative to the street and oncoming traffic. It helps you see if the screen is a head-on read, angled at an intersection, or parallel to the road.
- **Traffic Flow Analysis:** By looking at how the blue cone overlays onto the street map, media buyers can accurately analyze which specific lanes, pedestrian walkways, or approaches are captured by the billboard. For instance, you can visually confirm if a screen specifically targets cars waiting at a red light or vehicles driving toward a major shopping mall entrance.
