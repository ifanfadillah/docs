This page breaks down the Configuration & Screen Availability parameters (Unit, Slot, and Spot) and how they function in the real-world context of Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH) advertising:

## Unit (Total Unit)

This indicates the total number of physical screens available at a specific location.

![Unit](/images/unit.png)

A standard roadside digital billboard typically has a Unit count of 1. However, if you are purchasing inventory in a transit hub, shopping mall, or lamp pole network, the Unit count might be much higher (e.g., 20 units). Buying a multi-unit location means your advertisement will be distributed across that entire local network of screens, dramatically increasing your physical footprint and audience reach in that specific area.

---

## Slot

This refers to the total number of advertisement slots available within the digital screen's rotation cycle (often referred to as a "loop" or "playlist").

<Frame>
  ![Slot](/images/slot.png)
</Frame>

DOOH screens are generally shared environments. If a screen is configured with a "Max Slot" of 6, it means the screen rotates through 6 different advertisements consecutively. When you buy a slot, you are securing one of those 6 positions. This metric dictates your "Share of Voice" (SOV)—a screen with fewer maximum slots means your advertisement will cycle back and appear more frequently.

---

## Spot

A spot represents the actual, individual instance of your advertisement playing on the screen. While a "Slot" is your reserved space in the line, the "Spot" is the physical playback of your ad.

- **Video Duration per spot:** The precise length of time your creative will be displayed each time it appears in the rotation. In the DOOH industry, this is commonly restricted to fixed durations, such as 15 or 30 seconds, to keep the loop moving.
- **Total Spot per slot:** This is the calculated number of times your ad will successfully play within a given period. This is determined by dividing the screen's daily Operational Hours by the total duration of the loop. For example, your reserved slot might guarantee you 360 total spots (plays) per day. This metric is crucial because more spots directly translate to higher potential exposures (Impressions) for your campaign.
