title: "What is Campaign"
---

As an Agency or Media Buyer, you are surely familiar with the term **Campaign**. A campaign represents a strategic media buying initiative where you plan, execute, and monitor your advertising efforts across various Out-of-Home (OOH) inventories. The Campaign feature is your central command center to create new ad buys, schedule, and manage ongoing performance for one or more of your clients and brands.

This page helps you understand the various menus and data points available in the Campaign feature dashboard on Playlog Campaign Report Modules.

## Accessing Campaign Feature

You can access the campaign feature by click the '**Campaign**' Menu in the Navigation Bar

![Accessing Campaign 1](/images/accessing-campaign-1.png)

Then it will display the **Campaign Page.**

![Accessing Campaign 1 2](/images/accessing-campaign-1.2.png)

## Campaign Overview

![Campaign Overview](/images/campaign-overview.png)

Based on the **Campaign Overview** above, here is a detailed explanation of each section to help you manage your media pipeline:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Total Campaign">
    Displays the cumulative number of all campaigns you are currently managing within your account, encompassing every client, brand, and historical buy you have recorded in the system.

    ![Campaign Total](/images/campaign-total.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Total Not Started Campaign">
    Displays the number of campaigns that are currently in the drafting phase, awaiting approval, or scheduled for a future flight date. Monitoring this ensures your upcoming media launches are properly queued and ready to go live on time.

    ![Campaign Not Started](/images/campaign-not-started.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Total Running Campaign">
    Displays the number of active campaigns that are currently live on the screens. These are the campaigns actively generating impressions, reaching your target audience, and utilizing your allocated daily budgets.

    ![Campaign Running](/images/campaign-running.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Total Finished Campaign">
    Displays the number of campaigns that have successfully completed their scheduled flight dates. These campaigns are now paused from running and are ready for post-buy analysis, final reporting, and client billing.

    ![Campaign Finished](/images/campaign-finished.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by Campaign Status">
    You can filter your dashboard to quickly isolate campaigns based on their current operational health or lifecycle stage (e.g., Draft, Running, Paused, or Completed) ([See Campaign Status ↗](/status)).

    ![Campaign Filter By Campaign Status](/images/campaign-filter-by-campaign-status.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search Campaign">
    A quick search bar that allows you to instantly locate a specific media buy by typing in the campaign name, client brand, or specific keywords used during the initial setup.

    ![Campaign Search](/images/campaign-search.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by Date Period">
    You can filter the campaign data to isolate buys that ran during a specific timeframe or quarter. Ensure you input the dates in the format `dd/MM/yyyy ~ dd/MM/yyyy` to align with your client's reporting periods.

    ![Campaign Filter By Date](/images/campaign-filter-by-date.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add New Campaign Button">
    To initiate a new media buy in the system, simply click the “Add New” button. The system will immediately take you to the creation workflow ([See Create Campaign ↗](/create-campaign-1)) where you must fill in the crucial details, including the campaign objectives, flight dates, target inventory, and creative assets.

    ![Campaign Add New Button](/images/campaign-add-new-button.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="List Campaign">
    In this section, you can see a high-level summary of all the campaigns you have successfully entered into the portal. This table allows media buyers to monitor the status of their media plan at a glance.

    ![Campaign List](/images/campaign-list.png)

    The campaign properties that can be seen in this table are as follows:

    - **Campaign Name:** The designated name of your initiative.
    - **Start Date & End Date:** The agreed-upon flight period for the ads to run.
    - **Remaining Days:** A real-time countdown of how many days are left in the active flight period.
    - **Campaign Status:** The current operational state of the campaign ([See Campaign Status ↗](/status)).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit & Delete Action Button">
    Using the action buttons located on the right side of the list, you can seamlessly navigate to the Edit Page ([See Edit a Campaign ↗]()) to make necessary adjustments to your flight dates or targeting, or you can delete a draft campaign ([See Delete a Campaign ↗]()) that is no longer proceeding.

    ![Campaign Action Button](/images/campaign-action-button.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pagination">
    The sequential numbers in the image below are navigation points you can use to browse through multiple pages of your campaign list, particularly useful when managing a high volume of client accounts.

    ![Campaign Pagination](/images/campaign-pagination.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Page List">
    You can adjust the density of the campaign list displayed on your screen. By default, 10 items are displayed per page, but you can expand this to show 50 or 100 items for a broader overview of your media buys.

    ![Campaign Page List](/images/campaign-page-list.png)
  </Step>
</Steps>
