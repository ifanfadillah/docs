---
title: "What is Explore"
---

Within the Playlog ecosystem, the **Explore** feature is your interactive, map-based discovery engine. It is designed to help media buyers visually search, evaluate, and select Out-of-Home (OOH) and Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH) inventory across Indonesian area before adding them to a campaign. The explore feature visualizes the real-world context of your potential ad placements.

## Explore Map

![Explore Overview](/images/explore-overview.png)

Based on the **Explore Overview** above, here is a detailed explanation of each section to help you placement your ads:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Main Interactive Map">
    This is the core workspace of the Explore module. It provides a geographical overview of all available Out-of-Home (OOH) and Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH) inventory. When zoomed out, the map groups billboards into color-coded, numbered clusters to give you an immediate sense of inventory density across different regions.

    ![Explore Map](/images/explore-map.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Zoom Level Indicator">
    Displays the current numeric zoom level of your map view. This helps you understand how far you are drilled down into a specific geographic area (by default Zoom Level: 4).

    ![Explore Zoom Level](/images/explore-zoom-level.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Zoom Controls">
    Standard map controls that allow you to manually zoom in closer to inspect individual streets or zoom out for a deep view.

    ![Explore Zoom Control](/images/explore-zoom-control.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Total Inventories Count">
    Located at the top right, this dynamically displays the total number of available OOH/DOOH screens. As you move the map or apply filters, this number updates in real-time to reflect the matching inventory in your current view.

    ![Explore Total Inventories](/images/explore-total-inventories.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter POI (Point of Interest)">
    This tool allows you to target billboards based on their proximity to specific locations (like malls, hospitals, or colleges).

    <Info>
      This feature is only available and clickable after you have filtered or selected a specific city (see Point of Interest (POI)).
    </Info>

    ![Explore Filter Poi](/images/explore-filter-poi.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Traffic Heatmap">
    Represented by the traffic light icon, this toggle overlays general traffic condition data onto the map, helping you understand the typical vehicle flow around potential billboard locations.

    <Info>
      This feature is only available and clickable after you have filtered or selected a specific city (see Traffic Heatmap)
    </Info>

    ![Explore Traffic Heatmap](/images/explore-traffic-heatmap.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Density Map">
    Represented by the hexagon icon, this activates the real-world mobility and traffic density overlay. It covers the map in a color-coded grid system so you can visually analyze the audience density surrounding specific ad placements.

    <Info>
      This feature is only available and clickable after you have filtered or selected a specific city (see Density Map).
    </Info>

    ![Explore Density Map](/images/explore-density-map.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filters Options">
    Clicking this opens the advanced filtering sidebar. Here, you can narrow down the thousands of available screens by setting specific parameters.

    ![Explore Filter Menu](/images/explore-filter-menu.png)

    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Price Range">
        Allows you to filter billboards based on your campaign budget or minimum **Rate Card** of Billboard (see Rate Card). You can input the minimum and maximum price limits in Indonesian Rupiah (IDR).

        ![Filter Price Range](/images/filter-price-range.png)
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Minimun Impressions">
        Used to find inventory based on daily performance targets. You can enter the minimum number of impressions per day (Impression/day) that a screen must achieve.

        <Info>
          Impression = The total number of exposures to your advertisement (see Impression)
        </Info>

        ![Filter Impression](/images/filter-impression.png)
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Minimum Reach">
        Used to filter billboards that meet your daily unique audience reach targets. You can type the minimum target number for the Reach/day metric.

        <Info>
          The number of unique individuals that will likely see your ads regardless of frequency (see Reach).
        </Info>

        ![Filter Reach](/images/filter-reach.png)
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Location ">
        Allows you to narrow down your screen search based on specific geographic areas. There is a dropdown menu that can be used to select the City or District level.

        Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use the location filter in the Explore module:

        <Steps>
          <Step title="Step 1: Open the Location Dropdown">
            ![Filter Location 1](/images/filter-location-1.png)
          </Step>
          <Step title="Step 2: Select a City or Regency">
            A menu will open displaying a list of cities and regencies. You can type in the search bar or scroll through the list. Check the box next to the area name (for example, _Kota Jakarta Pusat_) to select all inventory within that city.

            ![Filter Location 2](/images/filter-location-2.png)
          </Step>
          <Step title="Step 3: Select Specific Districts">
            For more detailed targeting, click the arrow (`>`) next to the city name to open the list of districts within it. You can check the specific areas relevant to your campaign (for example, only checking _Tanah Abang_, _Cempaka Putih_, _Menteng_, etc.).

            ![Filter Location 3](/images/filter-location-3.png)
          </Step>
          <Step title="Step 4: Apply and View Map Results">
            After you click the **Show Billboard** button, the system will process the filter. The map will automatically zoom in and highlight your selected geographic boundaries (indicated by a purple outlined area) and show detail explore with list of inventories of left side panel (see Explore Detail Overview).

            ![Filter Location 4](/images/filter-location-4.png)
          </Step>
        </Steps>
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Inventory Specification">
        You can use the dropdown menu to select the specific specification of the screen (see Specification, Type & Category).

        ![Filter Specification](/images/filter-specification.png)
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Inventory Category">
        You can use the dropdown menu to select the screen category (see Specification, Type & Category).

        ![Filter Category](/images/filter-category.png)

        You can use the dropdown menu to select the orientation, such as landscape or portrait.
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Inventory Orientation">
        You can use the dropdown menu to select the orientation, such as landscape or portrait.

        ![Filter Orientation](/images/filter-orientation.png)
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Inventory Size">
        There are input fields to determine the exact Width and Height dimensions in meters (m).

        ![Filter Size](/images/filter-size.png)
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Inventory Availability">
        This filter helps you ensure whether the screen still has empty slots available for booking.
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search Billboard">
    A direct search bar that allows you to instantly locate a specific screen by typing in its name, street location, or unique SKU.

    ![Explore Search](/images/explore-search.png)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Explore Detail & Inventory List

To show Explore Detail & List of Inventory You **must zoom until Level 13** or after you have **filtered** or **selected a specific city** for better experience.

![Explore Detail Overview](/images/explore-detail-overview.png)

Based on the **Explore Detail & List Overview** above, here is a detailed explanation of each section to help you placement your ads:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Inventory Count Indicator">
    This section displays the number of inventories currently filtered or visible in your list compared to the total number of available screens in the system (e.g., `1005 / 27380 inventories`). As you apply filters, move or zoom the map this number updates dynamically.

    ![Explore Detail Inventory Count](/images/explore-detail-inventory-count.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sort By Dropdown">
    A dropdown menu that allows you to organize the displayed list of billboards. You can use this to quickly sort the inventory cards by specific criteria, making it easier to find screens that align perfectly with your campaign goals.

    ![Explore Detail Sort](/images/explore-detail-sort.png)

    By clicking the dropdown, you can arrange the inventory using the following filters:

    - **Top Reach:** Organizes the list to prioritize screens that deliver the highest daily unique audience.
    - **Top Impression:** Sorts the inventory to display billboards generating the highest total daily exposures at the top.
    - **Lowest Price:** Arranges the list starting with the most budget-friendly screens based on their listed price.
    - **Highest Price:** Sorts the list starting with the most premium, high-cost screens.
    - **Lowest CPM:** Highlights the most cost-efficient inventory first by sorting them based on the lowest Cost Per Mille (cost per one thousand impressions).
    - **Highest CPM:** Displays the inventory with the highest Cost Per Mille at the top of the list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Inventory Card">
    This is a quick summary card representing an individual physical screen. It gives you an immediate snapshot of the asset's key details without needing to open a new page.

    ![Explore Detail Inventory Card](/images/explore-detail-inventory-card.png)

    The Inventory card typically includes:

    - A thumbnail photo of the billboard.
    - The billboard's name and location.
    - Rate Card (e.g., IDR per month) & CPM
    - Inventory Specifications (Size, Orientation, and Type like DOOH).
    - Estimated daily performance metrics (Impressions and Reach).
  </Step>
  <Step title="See Details Button">
    Clicking this button will open a more comprehensive view of that specific inventory billboard. This likely includes expanded specification and performance data (see What is Inventory).

    ![Explore Detail Button](/images/explore-detail-button.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add to List Button">
    This primary action button allows you to select the screen and save it to your Shortlist. Grouping inventories into a Shortlist here makes it much easier to convert them into a live media plan when you are ready to create your campaign (see Add Inventory to Shortlist).

    ![Explore Detail Add To List](/images/explore-detail-add-to-list.png)
  </Step>
</Steps>
